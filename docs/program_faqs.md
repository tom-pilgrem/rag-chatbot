# MIP Analytics Career Accelerator — Full Program FAQ

---

## About the Program

Q: What is the MIP Analytics Career Accelerator?
A: The MIP Analytics Career Accelerator is a 2-year immersive program that gives you hands-on training, mentorship, and real-world client experience to launch a career in data. It was previously known as The Data School Down Under, which MIP ran from 2018. In 2025 it was rebranded — same team, same people, same program.

Q: How long is the program?
A: The program runs for 24 months on a fixed-term contract. It begins with 8 weeks of intensive training, followed by a capstone project in week 9, and then a series of client placements for the remainder of the 2 years.

Q: How many people are in each cohort?
A: Each cohort has 6 to 8 people. The exact number depends on both client demand and the quality of applicants in that round.

Q: How often do intakes run?
A: There are two intakes per year — one starting in April and one starting in October.

Q: Where is the program based?
A: The program is based in Sydney CBD. During the 8-week training phase, all participants are on-site in the Sydney office. Client placements may be on-site at client offices, remote, or hybrid — this depends on how each client works.

## Salary and Benefits

Q: Do I get paid during training?
A: Yes, from day one. You receive a full salary throughout both the training and placement phases of the program.

Q: What is the salary?
A: The salary is $65,000 per annum including superannuation in year one, rising to $70,000 in year two.

Q: What benefits are included?
A: Benefits include a health and wellness program and an inclusive team culture. You also gain industry-recognised certifications throughout the program at no cost to you.

Q: Is it a permanent role?
A: No. It is a 24-month fixed-term contract. At the end of the program, MIP supports you in finding your next role.

## Training Phase

Q: What does the 8-week training phase involve?
A: Training takes place in person at the Sydney CBD office, Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm. Each week follows a theme — introducing a new concept or building on previous skills, progressing from foundational to more advanced topics. Training is a mix of instructor-led classroom sessions and hands-on practice.

Q: What technologies will I learn?
A: The core tools are Power BI, Microsoft Fabric, SQL, Tableau, and Alteryx. Throughout the program you will also have opportunities to learn additional skills and gain industry-recognised certifications.

Q: What happens on Fridays during training?
A: Every Friday is dedicated to a project — either individual, in pairs, or as a team. These projects are designed to develop soft skills alongside technical skills, including requirements gathering, project planning, prioritisation, and communication. Each Friday wraps up with a presentation to the cohort and the wider MIP business. These presentations are open to anyone in the company who wants to attend.

Q: Is there a capstone project?
A: Yes. Week 9 is a capstone project where you bring together everything learnt during the 8 weeks of training and put it into practice in a single end-to-end project.

Q: What if I struggle to keep up during training?
A: Our application process is designed to identify people who will do well, so falling behind technically is rare. If someone does need extra support, we work with them through individual coaching plans. Challenges tend to be on the soft skills side rather than the technical side — and no one has ever been let go during the training phase for not keeping up. No one is left behind.

Q: Can I do the program online or remotely?
A: No. The 8-week training phase is in-person at MIP's Sydney CBD office, Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm. You must be based in Sydney or able to relocate for the training phase. Client placements after training may be on-site, remote, or hybrid depending on the client — but the training itself cannot be done remotely.

## Client Placements

Q: What happens after the training phase?
A: After training and the capstone project, you move into the placements phase. You are placed with MIP's clients — Australia's leading organisations — working on real data projects alongside more senior MIP consultants.

Q: Where will I be working during placements?
A: Placements are generally on-site at client offices, but can also be remote or hybrid. MIP matches the working style of each client, so the location and hybrid balance will vary depending on the placement. We cannot guarantee a fixed location or hybrid arrangement.

Q: Will I be working independently on client sites?
A: Initially you work as part of a wider team alongside senior MIP consultants. Over time, as you build experience and confidence, you become more independent. The pace of that progression varies by person.

Q: How many different clients will I work with?
A: This varies. The placements phase is designed to give you exposure to a variety of industries, working styles, and types of data work. MIP's commercial and consulting teams work to match you to suitable placements based on your interests and development goals where possible.

## Support and Mentorship

Q: What support is available during the program?
A: There are two main support structures throughout the program.

The first is the POD network. When you join, you are assigned to one of four PODs — small groups made up of people at different stages of the program, led by a senior MIP consultant. PODs meet fortnightly, alternating between knowledge-sharing sessions (where associates share learnings from their placements) and social sessions such as lunches or group activities.

The second is monthly one-to-one check-ins with Tom, our Chief of Staff. These sessions cover your overall development, give you a space to raise issues or challenges, and help MIP understand your interests and where you want more exposure. Tom works with the consulting and commercial teams to try to match upcoming placements to your goals — though this cannot always be guaranteed.

Q: What if I have an issue with a placement?
A: You can raise any issues in your monthly check-in with Tom, or through your POD lead at any time. MIP takes wellbeing and development seriously and will work with you to find a resolution.

## After the Program

Q: What happens when the 2-year program ends?
A: MIP actively supports you as you roll off the program. There are three typical routes:

1. Join MIP as a consultant. This depends on availability of roles within the business at the time and is not guaranteed. Typically one or two people from each cohort of 6 to 8 stay on at MIP beyond the two years.

2. Join a client you were placed with. This is the most common route. Think of your client placements as extended job interviews — you build networks, demonstrate your skills, and get a feel for different industries. Whether a client role is available depends on their demand at the time and is not guaranteed.

3. Go to market. MIP supports you with CV writing, interview practice, and introductions to its wider network where possible to help you find a role in the broader job market.

Q: Does MIP guarantee a job at the end?
A: No. MIP does not guarantee a permanent role at the end of the program. However, graduates leave with two years of real-world consulting experience, a strong professional network, industry certifications, and active support from the MIP team in finding their next step.

## Who Should Apply

Q: Who is the ideal candidate?
A: MIP welcomes graduates, career changers, and anyone who wants to start a career in data. There is no requirement for a specific degree, university, or professional background. What matters is a genuine interest in working with data and the ability to think critically.

Q: Do I need a degree or technical background?
A: No. MIP does not look at your CV, degree, or university history. The application process is specifically designed to assess what you can do — not where you have been.

Q: What backgrounds tend to do well?
A: People from a very wide range of backgrounds have succeeded in the program. A common profile is someone who has had some exposure to data in their existing role, has developed a curiosity about analytics, but cannot find a pathway to move into data within their current company. Both graduates and career changers do well.

Q: Is this program suitable for complete beginners?
A: Yes. You do not need prior experience with Tableau, Power BI, or any other data tool. The program is designed to take you from wherever you are and build you into a well-rounded data consultant.

## The Application Process

Q: How do I apply?
A: The application process is task-based rather than CV-based. The steps are:

1. Download Tableau Public or Power BI Desktop (both free).
2. Find a real dataset you are passionate about.
3. Create a data visualisation that tells a compelling story.
4. Submit your Tableau Public URL or Power BI file via the application form.
5. Receive feedback from MIP and submit an improved second version.
6. If shortlisted, attend a video interview where you present your visualisation for 10 minutes.
7. The strongest candidates are then invited to a final interview with the wider MIP team.

Q: Do I need to send a CV?
A: No. MIP does not ask for a CV as part of the application. The visualisation task is the application.

Q: What dataset should I use for my visualisation?
A: Choose a dataset you are genuinely interested in — whether that is sport, politics, culture, health, or anything else. You will produce better work on a topic you care about. Good sources include Makeover Monday, Data is Plural, Kaggle, Our World in Data, Data.world, and data.gov.au.

Q: Can I use a fictitious or generated dataset such as Superstore, Churn, or HR data?
A: No. MIP does not accept applications based on fictitious, artificially generated, or tutorial datasets such as Superstore, Churn, or HR. These datasets make it very difficult to distinguish original work from reproduced tutorials, and they rarely lead to compelling visualisations. You must use a real dataset with real values. If your application uses one of these datasets, MIP will let you know and invite you to resubmit with a new visualisation.

Q: Can I follow an online tutorial for my application viz?
A: No. Tutorials are a great way to learn, but your application visualisation must be original work. Recreating someone else's visualisation will not be accepted. MIP wants to see your own analysis, insights, and creative choices.

Q: What makes a strong application visualisation?
A: A strong application is a combination of three things: visual design, interactivity, and analytical storytelling. The visualisation should look compelling, allow the viewer to explore the data, and communicate a clear insight or narrative. MIP is looking for passion, creativity, and a flair for data — not perfection.

Q: What happens after I submit my first visualisation?
A: MIP reviews every application and replies with personalised feedback and suggestions for improvement. You then submit a revised second version. It is this improved version that MIP cares about most — so do not worry about how good the first submission is. Just send it and iterate from there.

Q: What if my application is not successful?
A: MIP never says never. Many successful applicants applied two or even three times before being accepted. Every application receives feedback, so you will always learn something from the process. You are encouraged to apply again for a future cohort.

## Visa and Work Rights

Q: Does MIP offer visa sponsorship?
A: No. In order to be considered for this program, you will need full working rights in Australia for the length of the program. Unfortunately, MIP cannot sponsor visas for this position.

Q: I am on a student visa or temporary visa — can I apply?
A: You need full working rights in Australia for the entire 24-month duration of the program. If your current visa does not cover this, you are not eligible to apply at this time.
